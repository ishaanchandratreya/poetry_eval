 No praise to thee, but what in you is writ,
 Not making worse what nature made so clear,
 And such a beauty as you master now.
 So all their parts of me untrue,
 My name be buried where my body is,
 And live no more to shame nor me nor you.
 For I am the wits of former days,
 To subjects worse have given admiring praise.
 Like as the marigold at the sun's eye,
 And in some antique book,
